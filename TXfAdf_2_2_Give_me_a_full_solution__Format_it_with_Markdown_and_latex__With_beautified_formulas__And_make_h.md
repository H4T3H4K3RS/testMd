

### **Solution**

The given equation is:

$$2x^2 + 2 = 0$$

Using Quadratic Formula,

$$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$

By substituting the values,

$$x = \frac{-(2) \pm \sqrt{(2)^2 - 4(2)(0)}}{2(2)}$$

$$x = \frac{-2 \pm \sqrt{4}}{4}$$

$$x = \frac{-2 \pm 2}{4}$$

$$x = \frac{-1}{2} \quad or \quad \frac{1}{2}$$

Hence,

$$x = -\frac{1}{2} \quad or \quad \frac{1}{2}$$
